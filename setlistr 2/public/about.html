About Setlistr

Building the performance
data layer for music.

Streaming has Spotify analytics. Radio has Mediabase and Nielsen. Live music — the fastest-growing segment of the entire music industry — has no equivalent. Until now.

The Problem

Live music generates billions in royalties but lacks reliable performance data.

Every time a song is performed live in a venue with a blanket PRO license, the songwriter is owed a performance royalty. The venues pay. The PROs collect. But without accurate setlist data, no one knows who to pay.

Most performances are never reported. Manual setlist submission is inconsistent, incomplete, and often weeks delayed. ASCAP and BMI auto-track only the top 300 grossing tours. Everyone else self-reports — and most don't bother.

When royalties sit unclaimed for three years, PROs redistribute them using market share formulas — not actual performances. The money goes to whoever has the most market share, not whoever actually performed.

The Opportunity

Streaming and radio have analytics infrastructure. Live music does not.

Setlistr uses AI-powered composition matching to identify songs at the composition level — not the recording level. This matters because royalties are paid to compositions, not recordings.

A live performance of "Fast Car" is Tracy Chapman's composition whether it's performed by Tracy Chapman, Luke Combs, or a folk singer in a 200-cap venue. Recording fingerprinting fails in these cases. Composition matching doesn't.

Technical Approach

We identify compositions, not performances.

We match

LyricsPhonetic matching of sung lyrics, accounting for live vocal variation.
Melodic patternsCore melodic structure, independent of key or tempo changes.
Structural signalsVerse-chorus structure, harmonic progression, rhythmic patterns.

We don't match

RecordingsWe don't identify specific studio versions or track releases.
ArrangementsLive instrumentation, tempo, and arrangement variations are ignored.
PerformersArtist identity comes from venue/tour data, not audio analysis.

This distinction is why Setlistr works where recording fingerprinting fails.

Trust & Compliance

Built with privacy and accuracy at the core.

No audio stored

We generate and store only acoustic fingerprints, not audio files. No performance recordings exist on our servers.

Artist approval required

No performance data is submitted to PROs without artist verification and approval.

PRO workflow integration

We format data to PRO specifications and provide audit-grade documentation for every submission.

Human review layer

All automated matches include confidence scores. Low-confidence matches are flagged for human review before submission.
