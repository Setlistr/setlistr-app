The Problem

Live music runs on incomplete data.

73% of live performances are never reported to PROs. ASCAP and BMI only auto-track the top 300 grossing tours. Every other artist must self-report — and most don't. When royalties go unclaimed for three years, PROs redistribute them using internal market formulas, not actual performances.

The problem isn't that artists don't care. It's that until now, accurate setlist capture required human effort at every show on earth. That's a logistics problem. We solve it with software.

Technical Approach

We identify compositions, not recordings.

This distinction is what makes Setlistr technically defensible. Recording fingerprinting (Shazam, ACRCloud) fails in live environments — a live cover of "Fast Car" sounds nothing like Tracy Chapman's studio recording. Setlistr matches on three composition-level signals:

WE MATCH
Lyrics (phonetic matching)
Melodic patterns (key/tempo agnostic)
Structural signals (verse/chorus shape)
WE DON'T MATCH
Specific recordings
Studio arrangements
Artist voice identity

No audio is recorded or stored. Only acoustic fingerprints are processed in real time.
