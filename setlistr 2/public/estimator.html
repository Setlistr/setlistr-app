Common Questions

Why haven't I been paid already?

PROs like ASCAP and BMI only auto-track the top 300 grossing tours. Everyone else must self-report — and most don't. If your setlists aren't being submitted, the money is pooled and redistributed using market formulas, not your actual performances.

What counts as a "performance royalty"?

Every time a song you wrote is performed live in public, you're owed a performance royalty — whether you're the performer or not. This includes cover performances by other artists at venues with blanket PRO licenses.

How does Setlistr know what I played?

Setlistr uses AI composition matching to identify songs during the performance. Unlike audio fingerprinting, we match at the composition level — so a live cover of a song is still correctly identified as your composition, regardless of key or tempo.

When do royalties actually arrive?

PRO payment timelines vary by organization and territory — typically 9–18 months after the performance date. Setlistr tracks submission status and notifies you when payments are expected.
